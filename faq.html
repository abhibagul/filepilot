Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers about how FilePilot works, what it costs, and how it keeps your passwords safe.

What is FilePilot, in plain terms?

FilePilot is a free desktop app for moving files between your computer and your servers — over SFTP, FTP, S3, cloud drives, and more. There's also an optional team server, called the Vault, that lets a whole team share server access safely without ever passing passwords around.

Is it really free?

Yes. Both the desktop app and the team Vault are 100% free and open source under the MIT license. There are no paid tiers, no per-seat fees, no ads, and no tracking — even for large companies.

Do I need the Vault to use the app?

No. The desktop app works completely on its own for managing your own connections. The Vault is only for teams that want to share access centrally, control who can reach what, and keep an audit log.

Where are my passwords stored?

On your own device, encrypted. You set a Master Password when you first open the app, and FilePilot uses it to lock all your saved logins and keys with strong encryption. Your passwords are never uploaded and never sent to any outside server.

What if I forget my Master Password?

For your security, it can't be recovered — not even by us. FilePilot never stores or transmits it, which is exactly what keeps your saved logins safe if someone steals your computer. If you forget it, you simply set a new one and re-enter your connection details.

How can a team share server logins without exposing the passwords?

An admin adds each server login to the Vault, where it stays encrypted. When you're approved, the app pulls those logins over a secure connection and locks them on your own device. You can connect and transfer files normally — but the raw passwords are never shown to you or anyone else.

Which servers and protocols does it support?

SFTP (SSH), FTP, FTPS, and SCP for traditional servers; Amazon S3 (plus MinIO and Ceph); WebDAV for NAS devices and Nextcloud/ownCloud; and cloud drives like Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, and Azure. You can also reach servers through proxies or jump hosts, or add new types with plugins.

Which operating systems can I run it on?

Windows 10 and 11, macOS (Apple Silicon and Intel), and all major Linux distributions — from a single codebase, so it looks and works the same everywhere.

Does FilePilot track me or "phone home"?

No. There is zero telemetry, no analytics, no ad trackers, and no required account. Everything happens on your computer, or on your own private Vault server if you run one.

What happens to my transfers if my internet drops?

Nothing is lost. FilePilot notices the outage, holds any pending transfers in a queue, and picks up exactly where it left off the moment you're back online — no re-sending the parts that already finished.

Can I automate repetitive transfers?

Yes. You can write short automation scripts in plain JavaScript inside a safe sandbox, and run them on a schedule or automatically when you connect, disconnect, or open the app — handy for nightly backups or routine syncs.

Can we host the Vault on our own infrastructure?

Yes. The Vault is a standard Node.js server you can run on your own machines, in Docker, or in any cloud — fully on-premise if you need it. It works with SQLite for small teams or PostgreSQL / MySQL for larger setups, and your data never leaves your infrastructure.

How do I know the audit log wasn't quietly edited?

Every entry in the Vault's audit log is cryptographically linked to the one before it. If anyone changes or deletes even a single entry, the chain breaks — and a built-in check points to exactly where. That makes tampering impossible to hide.
